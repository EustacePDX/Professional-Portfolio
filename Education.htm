College:

- Bachelor of Science from Eckerd College

- Graduated December 31, 2014 with Honors Diploma

- Computer Science Major and 3.56 GPA

In the fall of 2011, during my first semester of college, I took a class called Survey of Computing (CS 110M). This was the first computer science course I had ever taken, and it left a great impact on me. In Survey of Computing I found that I had a deep passion for the field, the subjects were fascinating and the problems intriguing. While I found the subject was at times challenging, it was very much within my grasp given a little hardwork and perseverance.

Over the following three years I took many computer courses. During sophmore year it was Data Structures (CS 221N) and Computer Graphics (CS 450), during which I built a user input game based on the Java Swing libraries and a short animation redered in a program called Pov-Ray.

I took GUI design (CS 350), Programming languages (CS 320) and Web Development (CS 331) amoung others junior year. In those classes I built an extension to the Java API libraries for geometric calculations, devolped a website for a local community client, and created a Apple iPhone application demonstrating use of the NSTimer mechanism.

With the help of diligence and midnight oil I was able to complete my senior year in only one semester. In that time I built an additional website for another community client (QM 410), and served as a research assistant to my mentor, verifying calculations made using CUDA code on the GPU (CS 498).

Additionally I completed the Eckerd College honors program while taking several composition courses, a film course, and studied two forign languages, Chichewa (WTI 9G) and French (FR 101/102/201/202). Extra curricularly I was involved with the Outdoor club of Eckerd College my Freshman year until my professors selected me to work as a lab assistant in the Advanced Computing lab in my sophmore year, taking up that free time. And as always I continued to play ice hockey in the off-season.

During my time in college I learned a ton of technical knowledge that is applicable to the field. More importantly though I discovered a great passion of mine and made a life decision to follow it forward.

High School:

- High School Diploma from Aloha High School

- Graduated June 16, 2011 as a Salutitorian

- AP Scholar with Honor and 4.16 GPA

In 2007 I began my high school studies. I entered high school with an advanced credit in French 1 which I had earned in middle school. My freshman year I earned a 4.0 GPA while takeing the advanced placement human geography course. Sophmore year I received a 3.93 while taking AP European history. In junior year I recieved a 3.66 and took both AP US history and AP environmental studies. And finally senior year I earned a 3.93 while studing AP US government and AP micro/macro economics. Taken all together and weighted to accomidate the AP courses I finished high school with a 4.16 weighted GPA, 23/432 in my class and with 7 AP classes earned the AP scholar award.

During high school I played soccer freshman and sophmore year, then was involved with the Beaverton youth peer court junior and senior year, and additonally I played ice hockey.

As a whole I found the experience of high school trying but also very rewarding. Primarily I learned the meaning of hardwork and discipline in high school. The rigor and amount of work that I took on taught me the importance of preparing for tasks, following instructions carefully, and continuing to work until the task is complete.
